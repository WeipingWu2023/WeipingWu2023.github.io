This Communication Skill Can Transform Your Life

Created2026-04-10|Updated2026-04-11|英语乐园
|Post Views:

Summary

Link Text

The video explains that communication is not a natural talent but a learned skill that can transform your relationships by increasing trust and emotional connection. To become a more effective communicator, you can use the LUV Method:

L – Listen

The first step is simply to shut up and provide an empathetic presence. This means being fully present mentally and emotionally, rather than just being in the same room.

  • Remove distractions: Put away your phone and make eye contact.
  • The 3-second rule: After the other person finishes talking, wait three seconds before you respond. This “wait time” makes the other person feel smarter, builds trust, and often encourages them to share even deeper thoughts.

U – Understand

True understanding comes from looking at more than just the words being said. In fact, research suggests that 93% of emotional meaning in communication comes from non-verbal cues like body language and tone of voice.

  • Use your body: Face the person directly (square your shoulders) and nod to show you are engaged.
  • Reflective listening: Repeat back what you heard by saying, “What I’m hearing is…”. This tells the other person that they matter enough for you to want to get it right, which actually lowers their stress levels.

V – Validate

Validation means acknowledging that the other person’s feelings are legitimate and understandable, even if you do not personally agree with their opinion.

  • Stop fixing: Avoid the “savior complex” where you immediately try to solve their problems.
  • Ask, don’t tell: Instead of giving advice, ask open-ended questions like, “What do you need right now?”. This allows the person to feel seen and safe rather than judged.

The goal of the LUV method is to answer two unconscious questions every human asks during an interaction: “Do you see me?” and “Am I safe with you?”. When you use this method, the answer to both questions becomes “Yes”.
